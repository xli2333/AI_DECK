# Slide Layout: Logo Matrix & Partner Wall

**Category:** Text and Structure > Profiles and Comparison
**Use Case:** Ecosystem Mapping, Competitive Analysis, Partner Showcase
**Source:** BCGMOREMORE_Part7 (Page 250)

## 1. Context and Goal
This slide provides a visual "Who's Who" of a market or ecosystem. By arranging partner or competitor logos into a structured grid (matrix), it conveys scale, diversity, and strategic relationships at a glance. It's often used to show a company's "Partnership Network" or "Competitive Benchmarking".

## 2. Visual Structure (Layout Description)
A grid-based matrix with categorized sections.
*   **Categories (Rows/Columns):** Group the logos by type (e.g., "Tier 1 Partners", "Technology Vendors", "Regional Competitors").

### Detailed Element Breakdown

#### A. Category Headers
*   **Style:** Small, bold labels placed at the top of each grid section or to the left of each row.
*   **Divider Lines:** Use thin grey lines to separate the categories.

#### B. The Logo Grid
*   **Spacing:** Ensure equal "padding" around every logo. Logos should not touch each other.
*   **Sizing:** All logos should be scaled to a similar "visual weight". (Small square logos might need more space, long horizontal logos might need to be centered).
*   **Container:** Place each logo inside a subtle white or light grey box to create a "card" effect, ensuring a uniform look regardless of the logo's original shape.

#### C. Highlights / Tags
*   **Badges:** Place small colored dots or "badges" in the corner of certain logos to indicate a status (e.g., Green dot = "Signed Partner", Yellow dot = "Target").
*   **Overlay:** Use a subtle semi-transparent grey overlay on certain logos to indicate they have been "excluded" or "superseded".

#### D. Bottom Action Bar (Optional)
*   **Content:** A summary of the ecosystem strength (e.g., "Largest partner network in the region with 50+ vendors").

## 3. Key Design Instructions (For AI Generation)
*   **Cleanliness:** This is the most important factor. The grid must be perfectly aligned.
*   **Color Balance:** Since logos come in many colors, use a neutral grey/white slide background to avoid visual clashing.
*   **Transparency:** Use PNG logos with transparent backgrounds where possible to avoid "white box" syndrome.
